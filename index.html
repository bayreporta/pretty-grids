Family Child Care Facilities

Between 2006 and 2012, 84 percent of California counties — 49 — saw a decline in the number of licensed familiy child care facilities. Only six counties — Lake, Santa Clara, Santa Cruz, Sierra, Tulare, and Yuba — saw an increase.

Child Care Centers

Between 2006 and 2012, 50 percent of California counties — 29 — saw an increase in the number of licensed child care centers, while 36 percent — 21 — saw a decline.

Between 2006 and 2012, 36 percent of California's counties — 21 — experienced a decline in both the number of licensed family child care facilities and child care centers. The counties hit hardest were mainly rural but also include major urban counties including Los Angeles, Sacramento and Contra Costa. Meanwhile, only 5 percent — 3 — had an increase in both, and those counties were Santa Clara, Santa Cruz and Tulare. Overall, the number of family child care facilies declined by 13 percent statewide, while the number of the child care centers increased by a modest one percent during that time period.
